# M5 — Event-to-Meeting Validation

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Status | Conditional — begins after M4 |
| Outcome | Decide whether curated micro-events create repeatable meeting conversion |
| Primary Epic | [Event to Meeting](../epics/EPIC-02_EVENT_TO_MEETING.md) |
| Experiment | [Professional Singles Pilot](../experiments/PROFESSIONAL_SINGLES_PILOT.md) |

## Milestone intent

M5 is an evidence checkpoint, not a Feature completion list. It asks whether a small recurring event
can create trusted local density and progress private interest into real meetings without
unacceptable safety, privacy, or operating cost.

## Entry criteria

- M4 is signed off.
- Success, failure, and continue-testing thresholds are frozen.
- The city, cohort, host, safety owner, and event formats are selected.
- Recruitment and event operations can run even if product automation fails.

## Exit criteria

- [ ] More than one recurring event has completed; a single event is not treated as proof.
- [ ] Invitation, RSVP, attendance, shortlist, mutual-interest, follow-up, confirmation, and
  completion data are reviewable at member and cohort level.
- [ ] Qualitative interviews cover value, authenticity, pressure, privacy, and safety.
- [ ] Every safety incident has an owner, disposition, and retrospective.
- [ ] Member acquisition source, operating hours, direct cost, and host workload are recorded.
- [ ] Results are compared with the thresholds chosen before launch.
- [ ] The team records one explicit decision: Continue, Iterate, Pivot, or Stop.

## Required evidence

- Invite-to-RSVP and show-up rate.
- Shortlists and mutual interests per attendee.
- Follow-up within seven days.
- Confirmed and completed meetings per event and per activated member.
- Median event-to-confirmed-meeting time.
- Second meeting or continued contact.
- Repeat attendance and referral.
- Reports, blocks, incidents, removals, cancellations, and cohort churn.
- Member interviews and operator notes.

## Decision interpretations

### Continue

Productize the narrowest operational bottleneck with evidence of repeated demand and meeting
conversion.

### Iterate

Repeat the experiment with one pre-declared change to cohort, event format, positioning, privacy,
or follow-up.

### Pivot

Retain the shipped nearby-discovery contract and test Relationship Activation independently.

### Stop

Do not manufacture activity through a feed or paid acquisition when the event-to-meeting outcome
did not occur.

## Non-goals

- National or multi-city scaling.
- Optimizing public engagement.
- Treating RSVP or attendance alone as product-market fit.
- Building the entire Events roadmap before the evidence review.
